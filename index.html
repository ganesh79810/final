The Problem

Riverton's legacy parking system uploads permit data every 2–4 hours. During that gap, officers write tickets to residents whose permits are already approved but invisible on their devices.

Section 02

The Dataset

1,200 synthetic records benchmarked against IPMI (2022) and Deloitte (2021) standards. The dataset encodes the causal mechanism — latency drives errors — not just the symptoms.

Generation Rules

  • Device_Sync_Latency_Minutes drawn from weighted distribution: 0 min (5%), 15 min (8%), 30 min (12%), 60 min (18%), 90 min (20%), 120 min (17%), 180 min (10%), 240 min (6%), 300 min (4%)
  • Erroneous_Citation probability = 0.05 + latency × 0.003, capped at 0.90 — calibrated to IPMI benchmark range of 20–35% for batch-upload systems
  • Dispute_Outcome conditioned on error flag: erroneous → 70% Dismissed; valid → 10% Dismissed
  • Admin_Handling_Time drawn from Normal(mean=20, sd=5) minutes — applied only to dispute records
  • All 1,200 records passed 4 structural validation constraints: date ordering, dispute integrity, IPMI range checks, handling time bounds

Dashboard Insights

Five-page interactive analytics dashboard. Every chart is tied to a DPAD-defined analysis variable. Use the tabs to navigate between pages.

Open Full Screen ↗
Dose-Response Relationship

Every additional 30 minutes of sync delay adds ~8–10 percentage points to the erroneous citation rate. At 0 min: ~5% errors. At 180+ min: >40% errors. One variable explains everything.

City-Wide — Not Local

All 4 zones average latency above 90 minutes with error rates between 34% and 38%. No zone is safe. This rules out zone-specific causes and confirms the batch architecture is the culprit.

Structural — Not Seasonal

Monthly citation trends show no significant variation across all 12 months of 2024. The error rate is constant because the cause is constant: the batch upload schedule never changes.

59.8% of Disputes Are Preventable

327 of 547 disputes filed are dismissed — confirmed wrongful citations. Each took ~20 minutes to resolve. Fix the latency, and the majority of this administrative burden disappears.

Section 04

The ParkSync Solution

ParkSync replaces the 2–4 hour batch upload with an event-driven real-time permit push. The moment an approval happens, every officer device knows.

📋

Permit Management

View, filter, and approve all permit records. 789 approved, 185 pending, 226 expired. New applications trigger immediate device push on approval.

🎫

Citation Records

All 1,200 citations filterable by erroneous flag, zone, latency range, and payment. Color-coded latency: green (0 min), amber (1–60 min), red (60+ min).

⚖️

Dispute Review

898 disputes managed in a full case-file interface. Supervisors open, review, and decide each case with full citation and permit history visible.

📊

Analytics Module

Real-time KPI dashboard inside the prototype: wrongful rate, avg latency, dispute rate, admin time per case — all calculated live from the 1,200-record dataset.

⚡

Real-Time Sync

Event-driven permit push replaces batch CSV export. Device updates within 2 minutes of permit approval. The synchronization gap is eliminated entirely.

Section 07

Outcomes & Next Steps

What this project proves — and what Riverton should do next.

01
Latency is the root cause
A single variable — Device_Sync_Latency_Minutes — explains 35.9% wrongful citations across all 4 zones and all 12 months. The problem is one architectural decision, not many.
02
The fix is structural, not expensive
ParkSync uses the existing permit database. No new hardware, no new zones. Real-time push instead of batch pull — total additional infrastructure cost: $0.00.
03
Pilot Downtown, then city-wide
Recommended: 90-day pilot in the Downtown zone (30% of citations). Run the KPI dashboard against live data. Evaluate ECR, DSL, and DRR against Dataset 1 baselines before full rollout.
